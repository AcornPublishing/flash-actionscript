The TextField._highquality property sets the amount of anti-aliasing applied to a movie

Description

The TextField._highquality property sets the amount of anti-aliasing applied globally throughout the SWF.

 

The textField._highquality value is not applied to the current text field.

Examples and practical uses

The TextField._highquality property controls the amount of anti-aliasing applied to a SWF (and hence the text fields inside the movie). Anti-aliasing provides some smoothing of edges, hence making edges and curves look smoother and higher in quality. Normally, the amount of anti-aliasing is controlled by the global_highquality property of a text field, but the TextField._highquality property can override the global setting (but still acts on the whole SWF). There are three levels of anti-aliasing available:

 

·       0 – no anti-aliasing (low quality)

·       1 – anti-aliasing except in animation (high quality)

·       2 – anti-aliasing and bitmap smoothing always on (best quality)

 

To set a TextField._highquality property , assign a value to the property like this:

 

myTextField ._highquality = 1;

 

Anti-aliasing levels can strongly affect the legibility of text, especially at small font sizes. Unfortunately, you cannot set the level of anti-aliasing per text field, but you can reduce the need for Flash to anti-alias at all by using pixel snap. See also the Tips and precautions sections for Button._highquality , which looks at this area in detail.

Tips and precautions

The TextField._highquality property lets you control the quality of a text field by changing the way anti-aliasing is applied. The more anti-aliasing applied, the better the clip looks. However, applying anti-aliasing also involves processor overhead. Applying no anti-aliasing (TextField._highquality property set to 0) produces lower quality movies suitable for slower streams or non-critical animations (such as buttons). A setting of 1 applies anti-aliasing for bitmaps only if there is no animation involved. Otherwise, no anti-aliasing is applied. The highest setting for TextField._highquality property applies anti-aliasing at all times, regardless of the content and processor requirements.

 

Rendering text fields is one of the most processor intensive things you can make Flash do, particularly if you force a text field redraw every frame. For example, making Flash render the last sentence requires the drawing of approximately 2400 curves, assuming a typical _sans font (you can see this if you select some text and try to break it apart into its basic lines/fills with Modify > Optimise ). When creating pages with lots of text, particularly if it is dynamic, consider reducing the quality to a lower setting.

 

A good way to mask font quality without using anti-aliasing is to simply reduce the contrast between a text fields background color and its text color. Changing from black text on white background to dark gray text on light gray often works wonders with no loss of performance!
