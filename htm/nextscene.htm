nextScene()

 

This method is compatible with Flash 2 and later.

 

The nextScene() method moves the playhead to the first frame of the next scene and stops there.

Description

The nextScene() method moves the playhead to the first frame of the next scene in the timeline, and stops playback at that point.

Notes

 

nextScene();

 

 

Moves the playhead to the first frame in the next scene of a movie and stop the playback at that point.

 

 

Playback is stopped when the nextScene() method is called.

 

 

nextScene(2);

 

 

No frame number can be specified; see gotoAndStop() for the ability to specify frames.

 

 

 

 

Examples and practical uses

Scenes can be used to organize a movie into discrete components. The components can be logical sections (introduction, main body, credits) or descriptive sections (product overview, schematics, installing, and so on). Scenes are managed in the Scene panel (Window > Panels > Scene ) where you can add, delete, change, or duplicate scenes. You can also manipulate the order of scenes in a movie through this panel. When a Flash player plays a movie that has been divided into scenes, the scenes are played one after another in sequence. You can move quickly through the scenes using the nextScene() and prevScene() methods.

 

The nextScene() method is used to move the timeline’s playhead to Frame 1 of the next scene in the movie, and stop the playhead there. It is often tied to an action such as a user clicking on a button:

 

on (release) {

 nextScene();

 // a play method can be added, or any other actions performed

}

Tips and precautions

Scenes can be a useful way to organize a movie’s contents into sections, allowing you to differentiate parts of a large movie into sub-movies. For the most part, though, scenes are a leftover from earlier versions of Flash and are seldom useful in Flash MX or later movies. The exception is when a movie has a lot of tweened animations, in which case scenes can be a useful marker (although layer folders are a better way to manage tweened components).

 

Scenes are easy to work with using the Scenes panel. The nextScene() method lets you move to the next scene in a movie, but doesn’t allow you to move to specific scenes. To move to the previous scene, use the prevScene() method.

 

The nextScene() method does not return anything. See also the prevScene() method.
